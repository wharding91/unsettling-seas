Nancy Morejón (born 7 August 1944) is a Cuban poet, essayist, literary critic, and translator born and raised in a working-class neighborhood of Old Havana. She graduated with honors from the University of Havana in 1966 with a degree in Caribbean and French literature — the first Afro-Cuban to do so. She directed the Centro de Estudios del Caribe at Casa de las Américas and has translated major Caribbean writers including Aimé Césaire, Édouard Glissant, and Jacques Roumain into Spanish. In 2001 she became the first Black woman to win Cuba's National Prize for Literature.

Morejón's bibliography on this site gathers creative works from the 1989–2001 period. The selected works listed here foreground poetry represented in the local BibTeX files for the project.

## Literary Significance

Morejón is the most widely translated Cuban woman poet of the post-revolutionary era, whose poems "Black Woman" and "I Love My Master" are canonical texts of Afro-Latin American and feminist literary studies. She is a pivotal bridge between Anglophone and Francophone Caribbean writing and the Cuban literary tradition.
