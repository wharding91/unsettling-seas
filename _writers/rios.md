Soleida Ríos (born 1950) is a Cuban poet and experimental prose writer born in Santiago de Cuba. She began writing in the early 1970s during the ferment of the early revolutionary years. Over more than four decades she has maintained what she calls an Archivo de Sueños (Archive of Dreams), producing experimental texts that dissolve the boundaries between poetry, prose, and dream notation. Her work has been translated into English, Portuguese, and French.

Ríos's bibliography on this site gathers creative works from the 1989–2001 period. The selected works listed here foreground poetry and experimental prose represented in the local BibTeX files for the project.

## Literary Significance

Ríos is Cuba's foremost practitioner of experimental trans-genre writing, a two-time winner of the Cuban Critics' Prize whose formal innovations — fragmentary, oneiric, and refusing conventional genre categories — have influenced a generation of Latin American and Caribbean poets.
