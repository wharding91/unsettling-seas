Évelyne Trouillot (born 2 January 1954) is a Haitian novelist, short story writer, playwright, and poet born in Port-au-Prince. A member of the prominent Trouillot intellectual family, she studied languages and education in the United States before returning to Haiti in 1987, where she has since taught French at the State University. In 2002 she co-founded Pré-Texte, a writers’ organization supporting reading and writing workshops across Haiti. She writes in both French and Haitian Creole.

Trouillot’s bibliography on this site gathers creative works from the 1989–2001 period. The selected works listed here foreground fiction and poetry represented in the local BibTeX files for the project.

Literary Significance

Trouillot is the leading Haitian woman novelist writing in French today, whose fiction about slavery, the Duvalier dictatorship, and Haitian diaspora communities has won the Prix Carbet de la Caraïbe, the Prix Beaumarchais, and the Prix Soroptimist, making her work essential to the contemporary Francophone Caribbean literary canon.
