Georgina Herrera (23 April 1936 – 13 December 2021) was a Cuban poet, radio and television dramatist, and screenwriter born in Jovellanos, Matanzas. She moved to Havana at twenty, working first as a domestic servant, and was publishing poetry in Havana newspapers by age sixteen. After the 1959 revolution she joined the Novación Literaria movement and worked as a scriptwriter for the Cuban Institute for Radio and Television.

Herrera’s bibliography on this site gathers creative works from the 1989–2001 period. The selected works listed here foreground poetry represented in the local BibTeX files for the project.

Literary Significance

Herrera is the preeminent Afro-Cuban woman poet of her generation, centering Black female experience, African heritage, and the spirit of cimarronaje — slave resistance and flight — in a body of work recognized by the International Latino Book Award and Cuba’s Distinction for National Culture.
